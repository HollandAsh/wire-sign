Tilapia Cerviche

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds of fresh Tilapia filets
  • 1 large Cucumber
  • 4 Lemons
  • 6 Limes
  • 1 Large Red Onion
  • 2 Avocados
  • 5 or 6 stems of fresh Cilantro
  • Sea Salt and Black Pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. Wash the tilapia filets. Pat them dry. Then slice them into 1 inch pieces. Place the pieces in a large glass or plastic bowl.
  2. Cut the cucumber into cubes. Dice the red onion. Chop the cilantro finely. Add these into the bowl.
  3. Squeeze the lemons and limes into the bowl. Stir everything to mix the ingredients well in the citrus juice.
  4. Cut the avocado into chucks, mix them into the bowl.
  5. Add salt and pepper to taste.
  6. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap, and chill for 30 minutes to an hour.
  7. Serve!

Tips:

Be sure to use fresh seafood. That way you'll avoid food poisoning.

Curing the raw seafood in citrus juice "cooks" it. Making it safe to eat.

Once you finish making cerviche, refrigerate it for half an hour. It tastes great when served chilled.

Use a glass or stoneware bowl, a non-porous bowl. Don't use metal or wood because the citrus juice will react with the bowl.

Cerviche is best eaten shortly after it's prepared. If there are leftovers, refrigerate them right away. Eat any leftovers the next day, or throw them out.

I love to add a swirl of GochuJang sauce on top of each serving. It adds a touch of spicy and sweetness to the dish. Some versions of Cerviche are traditionally served with ketchup.

There are endless variations of Cerviche.
Contrasting colors of seafood and vegetables will look most appealing.
